Preface

Introduction

Social media is a modern tool, but Islam provides timeless principles that guide how it should be used. Every post, comment, like, and share falls under a person's actions — recorded and accountable before Allah. A Muslim must approach social media with consciousness of Allah (Taqwā), sincerity, and a sense of responsibility.

The Qur'an and Sunnah lay down clear principles about speech, behaviour, intention, modesty, and time. These principles apply to online activities just as fully as they apply offline. This booklet brings these timeless teachings into the context of the digital world, offering guidance for Muslims of all ages and backgrounds.

Foundation

Evidences on Using Social Media Islamically

Social media is a modern phenomenon, yet the Qur'ān and Sunnah contain timeless principles governing speech, honesty, modesty, time, and intention — all of which apply directly to how a Muslim uses these platforms.

Qur'ān 50:18 — Every word is recorded
مَّا يَلۡفِظُ مِن قَوۡلٍ إِلَّا لَدَيۡهِ رَقِيبٌ عَتِيدٌ
سورة ق، الآية ١٨
"Not a word does he utter except that there is an observer ready to record it."
Every comment, caption, post, and message typed online is included in this. Nothing is anonymous to Allah.
Qur'ān 49:6 — Verify before you share
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓاْ إِن جَآءَكُمۡ فَاسِقُۢ بِنَبَإٍ فَتَبَيَّنُوٓاْ أَن تُصِيبُواْ قَوۡمَۢا بِجَهَٰلَةٍ فَتُصۡبِحُواْ عَلَىٰ مَا فَعَلۡتُمۡ نَٰدِمِينَ
سورة الحجرات، الآية ٦
"O you who believe! If a wicked person comes to you with information, verify it, lest you harm people in ignorance and regret what you have done."
A direct command against spreading rumours, unverified news, or forwards that have not been fact-checked.
Qur'ān 49:12 — No backbiting
وَلَا يَغۡتَب بَّعۡضُكُم بَعۡضًاۚ أَيُحِبُّ أَحَدُكُمۡ أَن يَأۡكُلَ لَحۡمَ أَخِيهِ مَيۡتٗا فَكَرِهۡتُمُوهُۚ
سورة الحجرات، الآية ١٢
"Do not backbite one another. Would one of you like to eat the flesh of his dead brother? You would hate it."
Online gossip, trolling, and exposing others' faults on social media are forms of backbiting, explicitly forbidden here.
Qur'ān 24:30 — Lower the gaze
قُل لِّلۡمُؤۡمِنِينَ يَغُضُّواْ مِنۡ أَبۡصَٰرِهِمۡ وَيَحۡفَظُواْ فُرُوجَهُمۡۚ ذَٰلِكَ أَزۡكَىٰ لَهُمۡۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرُۢ بِمَا يَصۡنَعُونَ
سورة النور، الآية ٣٠
"Tell the believing men to lower their gaze and guard their chastity; that is purer for them. Indeed, Allah is All-Aware of what they do."
Lowering the gaze extends to the digital screen — inappropriate images, videos, and content must be avoided online.
Ḥadīth — Speak good or be silent
مَنْ كَانَ يُؤْمِنُ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ فَلْيَقُلْ خَيْرًا أَوْ لِيَصْمُتْ
"Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day should speak good or remain silent."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (6018) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (47)
This applies to posting, commenting, tweeting, sharing, and all online expression.
Ḥadīth — Actions are by intention
إِنَّمَا الْأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ وَإِنَّمَا لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مَا نَوَى
"Actions are judged by intentions, and every person will have what they intended."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (1) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (1907)
Even online activities must be done with sincere intention for Allah's pleasure. This transforms the ordinary into worship.
Ḥadīth — Do not waste time
نِعْمَتَانِ مَغْبُونٌ فِيهِمَا كَثِيرٌ مِنَ النَّاسِ: الصِّحَّةُ وَالْفَرَاغُ
"There are two blessings which many people waste: health and free time."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (6412)
Hours consumed by aimless scrolling is a waste of the blessing of free time — a matter of accountability on the Day of Judgement.

Chapter One

The Place of Social Media in Modern Life

The Advice

Social media has become a major part of modern life for people of all ages — used for communication, education, business, and entertainment. While it offers many benefits, it also carries risks such as distraction, spreading false information, and exposure to harmful content. A Muslim must understand that social media is a tool, not a purpose. It can be a means of reward if used correctly, or a source of sin if misused. Therefore, it should always be approached with responsibility, balance, and consciousness of Allah.

Qur'ān 28:77 — Balance between this world and the next
وَٱبۡتَغِ فِيمَآ ءَاتَىٰكَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلدَّارَ ٱلۡأٓخِرَةَۖ وَلَا تَنسَ نَصِيبَكَ مِنَ ٱلدُّنۡيَا
سورة القصص، الآية ٧٧
"And seek, through that which Allah has given you, the home of the Hereafter; but do not forget your share of the world."
This verse teaches balance. Social media is part of worldly life, but it should never distract from the Hereafter — the true purpose of our existence.
Qur'ān 103:1–3 — Time is a trust
وَٱلۡعَصۡرِ ۝ إِنَّ ٱلۡإِنسَٰنَ لَفِي خُسۡرٍ ۝ إِلَّا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ وَعَمِلُواْ ٱلصَّٰلِحَٰتِ وَتَوَاصَوۡاْ بِٱلۡحَقِّ وَتَوَاصَوۡاْ بِٱلصَّبۡرِ
سورة العصر، الآيات ١–٣
"By time, indeed mankind is in loss — except those who believe and do righteous deeds, and advise one another to truth, and advise one another to patience."
Spending long hours on social media without benefit leads to loss. When used for good, however, it can become a source of reward.
Qur'ān 50:18 — All words are recorded
مَّا يَلۡفِظُ مِن قَوۡلٍ إِلَّا لَدَيۡهِ رَقِيبٌ عَتِيدٌ
سورة ق، الآية ١٨
"Not a word does he utter except that there is an observer ready to record it."
Every post, comment, or message shared online is recorded. This should make a Muslim careful and intentional in all digital interactions.
Ḥadīth — Leave what does not concern you
مِنْ حُسْنِ إِسْلَامِ الْمَرْءِ تَرْكُهُ مَا لَا يَعْنِيهِ
"From the perfection of a person's Islam is leaving that which does not concern him."
📚 Jāmi' at-Tirmidhī (2317) — Ḥasan Authentic
Much of social media involves irrelevant or unnecessary content. A Muslim should avoid engaging in what is not beneficial or relevant to their life and dīn.
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْ لِي فِيمَا تَرَزَّقْتَنِي بَرَكَةً وَلَا تَجْعَلْهُ فِتْنَةً لِي
Allāhumma ij'al lī fīmā tarzaqtanī barakatan wa lā taj'alhu fitnatan lī
"O Allah, make what You have provided for me a source of blessing, and do not make it a trial for me."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Recognise that social media is a tool — it can be used for good or ill depending on the one using it.
  • 2 Use it for beneficial purposes: learning, da'wah, and maintaining good relationships.
  • 3 Avoid excessive use that leads to neglect of prayer, family, or responsibilities.
  • 4 Remember that every online action is recorded by Allah.
  • 5 Maintain balance between online activities and worship, work, and family life.
Understanding the true place of social media helps a Muslim use it wisely — transforming it from a distraction into a means of benefit, reward, and closeness to Allah.

Chapter Two

Using Social Media with the Fear of Allah

The Advice

Fear of Allah (Taqwā) should guide a Muslim in every aspect of life — including social media. Whether posting, commenting, liking, or sharing, a believer must remain conscious that Allah is always watching. Many people behave differently online because they feel hidden or anonymous, but a Muslim knows that nothing is hidden from Allah. Using social media with Taqwā means avoiding sinful content, maintaining good manners, and ensuring that all online actions are pleasing to Allah.

Qur'ān 4:1 — Allah is Ever Watchful
إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ عَلَيۡكُمۡ رَقِيبٗا
سورة النساء، الآية ١
"Indeed, Allah is Ever, over you, an Observer."
Even when using social media alone and in private, a Muslim must act with full awareness of Allah's presence.
Qur'ān 40:19 — Hidden browsing is known to Allah
يَعۡلَمُ خَآئِنَةَ ٱلۡأَعۡيُنِ وَمَا تُخۡفِي ٱلصُّدُورُ
سورة غافر، الآية ١٩
"He knows the stealthy glance of the eyes and what the hearts conceal."
This includes what a person secretly views online. Even private or incognito browsing is fully known to Allah.
Qur'ān 57:4 — Allah is with you wherever you are
وَهُوَ مَعَكُمۡ أَيۡنَ مَا كُنتُمۡۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعۡمَلُونَ بَصِيرٌ
سورة الحديد، الآية ٤
"And He is with you wherever you are, and Allah is All-Seer of what you do."
Whether online or offline, Allah's knowledge and awareness are constant. This must shape every digital interaction.
Ḥadīth — Be mindful of Allah wherever you are
اتَّقِ اللَّهَ حَيْثُمَا كُنْتَ، وَأَتْبِعِ السَّيِّئَةَ الْحَسَنَةَ تَمْحُهَا، وَخَالِقِ النَّاسَ بِخُلُقٍ حَسَنٍ
"Be mindful of Allah wherever you are; follow a bad deed with a good one and it will erase it; and behave with people with good character."
📚 Jāmi' at-Tirmidhī (1987) — Ḥasan Authentic
This hadīth directly applies to social media. Taqwā must be maintained in private chats, public posts, and all online behaviour.
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ خَشْيَتَكَ فِي الغَيْبِ وَالشَّهَادَةِ
Allāhumma innī as'aluka khashyataka fil-ghaybi wash-shahādah
"O Allah, I ask You to grant me fear of You in private and in public."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Before posting, ask yourself: "Would I do this if people could see me in front of a scholar or pious person?"
  • 2 Avoid viewing or sharing inappropriate or sinful content, even in private.
  • 3 Maintain good manners in all comments, messages, and replies.
  • 4 Do not use anonymity as an excuse for wrongdoing online.
  • 5 Make the intention before opening any social media app: "I use this for what pleases Allah."
  • 6 Seek forgiveness immediately after any online sin.
Using social media with the fear of Allah transforms it from a source of distraction into an act of 'ibādah and accountability, drawing a Muslim closer to their Creator.

Chapter Three

Sincerity of Intention in Online Activities

The Advice

Sincerity (Ikhlāṣ) is essential in every action, including what we do online. A Muslim should use social media with the intention of pleasing Allah, not seeking attention, praise, or popularity. Many online activities — posting, sharing, commenting — can easily become acts of showing off (Riyā'). Therefore, it is vital to purify one's intention and ensure all digital actions are done for a good purpose. When intention is sincere, even simple online actions can become acts of worship.

Qur'ān 98:5 — Worship must be sincere
وَمَآ أُمِرُوٓاْ إِلَّا لِيَعۡبُدُواْ ٱللَّهَ مُخۡلِصِينَ لَهُ ٱلدِّينَ حُنَفَآءَ
سورة البينة، الآية ٥
"And they were not commanded except to worship Allah, being sincere to Him in religion, inclining to truth."
All acts — including online activities — should be done purely for Allah's pleasure, not for people's approval or applause.
Qur'ān 18:110 — Do not associate anyone in your worship
فَمَن كَانَ يَرۡجُواْ لِقَآءَ رَبِّهِۦ فَلۡيَعۡمَلۡ عَمَلٗا صَٰلِحٗا وَلَا يُشۡرِكۡ بِعِبَادَةِ رَبِّهِۦٓ أَحَدَۢا
سورة الكهف، الآية ١١٠
"So whoever hopes for the meeting with his Lord — let him do righteous work and not associate in the worship of his Lord anyone."
This includes avoiding the showing off of good deeds, such as posting acts of charity or worship online purely to be seen and admired.
Ḥadīth — Actions are by intention
إِنَّمَا الْأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ وَإِنَّمَا لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مَا نَوَى
"Actions are judged by intentions, and every person will have what they intended."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (1) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (1907)
Every post, share, or comment is judged by its underlying intention. A sincere intention transforms ordinary online actions into worship earning reward from Allah.
Ḥadīth — The greatest fear: showing off
أَخْوَفُ مَا أَخَافُ عَلَيْكُمُ الشِّرْكُ الْأَصْغَرُ، قَالُوا: وَمَا الشِّرْكُ الْأَصْغَرُ؟ قَالَ: الرِّيَاءُ
"The thing I fear most for you is the minor shirk." They asked, "What is that?" He said: "Showing off (Riyā')."
📚 Musnad Aḥmad (23630) — Authenticated by scholars
Social media amplifies the danger of showing off. A Muslim must be especially vigilant not to seek likes, followers, or praise instead of Allah's pleasure.
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ أُشْرِكَ بِكَ شَيْئًا وَأَنَا أَعْلَمُ وَأَسْتَغْفِرُكَ لِمَا لَا أَعْلَمُ
Allāhumma innī a'ūdhu bika an ushrika bika shay'an wa anā a'lam, wa astaghfiruka limā lā a'lam
"O Allah, I seek refuge in You from associating anything with You knowingly, and I seek Your forgiveness for what I do unknowingly."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Before posting, ask yourself: "Am I doing this for Allah or for people?"
  • 2 Avoid displaying acts of worship, charity, or Qur'an recitation purely to gain praise online.
  • 3 Renew your intention regularly, especially before logging on to social media.
  • 4 Focus on sharing content that is genuinely beneficial, not just impressive.
  • 5 Be content with Allah's reward — it is more lasting than human approval.
  • 6 Seek forgiveness immediately if you notice pride or desire for attention entering your heart.
Sincerity in online activities turns social media into a means of reward. Without it, even good actions may lose their value — but with it, small actions become great in the sight of Allah.

Chapter Four

Using Social Media for Beneficial Purposes

The Advice

Social media is a powerful tool that can be used for good or ill. A Muslim should strive to use it for beneficial purposes: spreading knowledge, giving da'wah, helping others, sharing reminders of Allah, and learning useful skills. Every hour spent online can either be wasted or turned into a source of reward. Using social media with clear intention and purpose ensures it becomes a means of growth, contribution, and closeness to Allah — rather than distraction or harm.

Qur'ān 5:2 — Cooperate in righteousness
وَتَعَاوَنُواْ عَلَى ٱلۡبِرِّ وَٱلتَّقۡوَىٰۖ وَلَا تَعَاوَنُواْ عَلَى ٱلۡإِثۡمِ وَٱلۡعُدۡوَٰنِۚ
سورة المائدة، الآية ٢
"And cooperate in righteousness and piety, and do not cooperate in sin and aggression."
Sharing knowledge, good reminders, or helpful content online is a form of cooperation in righteousness, directly rewarded by Allah.
Qur'ān 6:160 — Good deeds are multiplied
مَن جَآءَ بِٱلۡحَسَنَةِ فَلَهُۥ عَشۡرُ أَمۡثَالِهَاۖ
سورة الأنعام، الآية ١٦٠
"Whoever comes with a good deed will have ten times the like thereof."
Sharing a beneficial reminder, a Qur'ānic verse, or an encouraging word online can earn tenfold reward from Allah.
Ḥadīth — The best of people benefit others
خَيْرُ النَّاسِ أَنْفَعُهُمْ لِلنَّاسِ
"The best of people are those who are most beneficial to others."
📚 Al-Mu'jam al-Awsaṭ (5787) — Ḥasan
Using social media to help, teach, guide, or uplift others aligns directly with the Prophet's ﷺ guidance on what makes a person excellent.
Ḥadīth — Convey even one verse
بَلِّغُوا عَنِّي وَلَوْ آيَةً
"Convey from me even if it is one verse."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (3461)
Social media provides a platform to share Qur'ān, Ḥadīth, and beneficial advice to thousands — fulfilling this prophetic directive.
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ انْفَعْنِي بِمَا عَلَّمْتَنِي وَعَلِّمْنِي مَا يَنْفَعُنِي وَزِدْنِي عِلْمًا
Allāhumma anfa'nī bimā 'allamtanī wa 'allimnī mā yanfa'unī wa zidnī 'ilmā
"O Allah, benefit me with what You have taught me, teach me that which will benefit me, and increase me in knowledge."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Share Qur'ānic verses, Ḥadīth, and beneficial Islamic reminders on your platforms.
  • 2 Post about charitable causes, educational content, or community initiatives.
  • 3 Use social media to learn skills, seek Islamic knowledge, and grow personally.
  • 4 Encourage others toward good and gently redirect away from harm.
  • 5 Avoid engaging in meaningless trends or harmful viral content.
  • 6 Make du'ā' that your online activity benefits both you and those who see it.
A Muslim who uses social media for beneficial purposes earns reward, spreads good, and fulfils their responsibility as a carrier of truth in their community.

Chapter Five

Guarding the Tongue in Online Speech

The Advice

The tongue — and its digital equivalent, the keyboard — is small but enormously powerful. A Muslim must guard their speech online by choosing words carefully, speaking only truth, and engaging with kindness. Typing without thinking often leads to sin and regret. Discipline in online speech reflects strong faith and protects one's character, relationships, and standing before Allah.

Qur'ān 50:18
مَّا يَلۡفِظُ مِن قَوۡلٍ إِلَّا لَدَيۡهِ رَقِيبٌ عَتِيدٌ
سورة ق، الآية ١٨
"Not a word does he utter except that there is an observer ready to record it."
Ḥadīth — Speak good or be silent
مَنْ كَانَ يُؤْمِنُ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ فَلْيَقُلْ خَيْرًا أَوْ لِيَصْمُتْ
"Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day should speak good or remain silent."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (6018) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (47)
Ḥadīth — A careless word leads to Hellfire
إِنَّ الْعَبْدَ لَيَتَكَلَّمُ بِالْكَلِمَةِ مَا يَتَبَيَّنُ فِيهَا يَهْوِي بِهَا فِي النَّارِ أَبْعَدَ مِمَّا بَيْنَ الْمَشْرِقِ وَالْمَغْرِبِ
"A person may utter a word without thinking about it, and because of it fall into the Hellfire farther than the distance between east and west."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (6477) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (2988)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ احْفَظْ لِسَانِي وَأَصْلِحْ قَوْلِي
Allāhumma iḥfaẓ lisānī wa aṣliḥ qawlī
"O Allah, guard my tongue and rectify my speech."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Pause before posting or commenting — ask: is this true, necessary, and kind?
  • 2 Avoid sarcasm, mockery, or harsh criticism in online discussions.
  • 3 Engage in disagreements with wisdom and good manners, not aggression.
  • 4 If you have nothing beneficial to add, choose silence.
  • 5 Use your platform for dhikr, encouragement, and beneficial advice.

Chapter Six

Avoiding Lies, Rumours & False Information

The Advice

The spread of lies, rumours, and unverified information online is one of the gravest dangers of social media. A Muslim is strictly forbidden from spreading anything without verification. Sharing false information — even unintentionally — can destroy reputations, spread panic, and cause real harm. Truth is a pillar of Islamic character; a Muslim must commit to accuracy and verification in every online share.

Qur'ān 49:6 — Verify before you share
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓاْ إِن جَآءَكُمۡ فَاسِقُۢ بِنَبَإٍ فَتَبَيَّنُوٓاْ
سورة الحجرات، الآية ٦
"O you who believe! If a wicked person comes to you with information, verify it, lest you harm people in ignorance."
Ḥadīth — It is enough of a lie to repeat everything you hear
كَفَى بِالْمَرْءِ كَذِبًا أَنْ يُحَدِّثَ بِكُلِّ مَا سَمِعَ
"It is sufficient as a lie for a man to narrate everything he hears."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim — Introduction (5)
Ḥadīth — Truthfulness leads to Paradise
عَلَيْكُمْ بِالصِّدْقِ فَإِنَّ الصِّدْقَ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْبِرِّ وَإِنَّ الْبِرَّ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ
"Hold fast to truthfulness, for it leads to righteousness, and righteousness leads to Paradise."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (6094) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (2607)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْنِي مِنَ الصَّادِقِينَ وَجَنِّبْنِي الْكَذِبَ
Allāhumma ij'alnī minaṣ-ṣādiqīn wa jannib-nil-kadhib
"O Allah, make me among the truthful and keep me away from lying."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Never share news, images, or videos without verifying the source.
  • 2 Avoid forwarding WhatsApp messages, tweets, or posts without checking their authenticity.
  • 3 If you shared something false, correct it publicly and promptly.
  • 4 Do not exaggerate facts to make content more engaging.
  • 5 Be especially cautious with content about scholars, leaders, or religious matters.

Chapter Seven

Avoiding Backbiting, Slander & Gossip Online

The Advice

Online platforms have made backbiting, slander, and gossip easier and more widespread than ever. Posting about someone's faults, sharing their private matters, or mocking them publicly are all serious sins in Islam. The Qur'ān compares backbiting to eating the flesh of a dead brother — a repulsive image to highlight its gravity. A Muslim must guard against these habits both in creating content and in consuming or sharing such content.

Qur'ān 49:12 — Backbiting is forbidden
وَلَا يَغۡتَب بَّعۡضُكُم بَعۡضًاۚ أَيُحِبُّ أَحَدُكُمۡ أَن يَأۡكُلَ لَحۡمَ أَخِيهِ مَيۡتٗا فَكَرِهۡتُمُوهُۚ وَٱتَّقُواْ ٱللَّهَۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابٌ رَّحِيمٌ
سورة الحجرات، الآية ١٢
"Do not backbite one another. Would one of you like to eat the flesh of his dead brother? You would hate it. Fear Allah; indeed, Allah is Accepting of repentance, Merciful."
Ḥadīth — The definition of backbiting
الْغِيبَةُ ذِكْرُكَ أَخَاكَ بِمَا يَكْرَهُ
"Backbiting is mentioning your brother in a way he would dislike."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (2589)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ احْفَظْ لِسَانِي مِنَ الْكَذِبِ وَالْغِيبَةِ وَالنَّمِيمَةِ
Allāhumma iḥfaẓ lisānī minal-kadhib wal-ghībah wan-namīmah
"O Allah, protect my tongue from lies, backbiting, and tale-bearing."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Do not post, share, or comment on content that exposes another person's faults or private matters.
  • 2 Avoid participating in cancel culture, pile-ons, or public shaming.
  • 3 If you see others being backbitten online, do not engage or amplify.
  • 4 Delete content you have posted that falls into backbiting and seek forgiveness.
  • 5 Unfollow or mute accounts that habitually engage in gossip and slander.

Chapter Eight

Respecting Others & Avoiding Online Abuse

The Advice

Respect for others is a fundamental Islamic principle. Online abuse, insults, harassment, and cyberbullying are prohibited in Islam, as they cause real harm to real people. A Muslim must embody the noble character of the Prophet ﷺ in all interactions — including online ones — treating others with dignity, fairness, and compassion regardless of differences in opinion, background, or belief.

Qur'ān 49:11 — Do not mock or insult others
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ لَا يَسۡخَرۡ قَوۡمٌ مِّن قَوۡمٍ عَسَىٰٓ أَن يَكُونُواْ خَيۡرٗا مِّنۡهُمۡ
سورة الحجرات، الآية ١١
"O you who believe! Let not a people ridicule [another] people; perhaps they may be better than them."
Ḥadīth — The Muslim does not harm others
الْمُسْلِمُ مَنْ سَلِمَ الْمُسْلِمُونَ مِنْ لِسَانِهِ وَيَدِهِ
"A Muslim is the one from whose tongue and hand other Muslims are safe."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (10) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (41)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ اهْدِنِي لِأَحْسَنِ الْأَخْلَاقِ لَا يَهْدِي لِأَحْسَنِهَا إِلَّا أَنْتَ
Allāhumma ihdinī li-aḥsanil-akhlāq, lā yahdī li-aḥsanihā illā anta
"O Allah, guide me to the best of character, for none can guide to the best of it except You."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Never respond to provocation online with insults or abuse.
  • 2 Do not engage in cyberbullying, trolling, or coordinated harassment.
  • 3 Disagree respectfully — attack ideas, not people.
  • 4 If you have wronged someone online, apologise sincerely and make it right.
  • 5 Report genuine online abuse and protect those being harmed.

Chapter Nine

Lowering the Gaze from Unlawful Content

The Advice

The command to lower the gaze applies fully to the digital world. Unlawful images, videos, and content are widespread on social media, and a Muslim must exercise conscious discipline in avoiding them. What the eyes see influences the heart — and a heart corrupted by haram content becomes distant from Allah. Protecting the gaze online is an act of worship that earns purity of heart and reward from Allah.

Qur'ān 24:30
قُل لِّلۡمُؤۡمِنِينَ يَغُضُّواْ مِنۡ أَبۡصَٰرِهِمۡ وَيَحۡفَظُواْ فُرُوجَهُمۡۚ ذَٰلِكَ أَزۡكَىٰ لَهُمۡ
سورة النور، الآية ٣٠
"Tell the believing men to lower their gaze and guard their chastity; that is purer for them."
Ḥadīth — The adultery of the eyes
الْعَيْنَانِ تَزْنِيَانِ وَزِنَاهُمَا النَّظَرُ
"The eyes commit adultery, and their adultery is the lustful look."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (6243) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (2657)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ احْفَظْ فَرْجِي وَاغْضُضْ بَصَرِي
Allāhumma iḥfaẓ farjī waghḍuḍ baṣarī
"O Allah, guard my chastity and help me lower my gaze."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Use content filters and parental controls to limit exposure to inappropriate material.
  • 2 Unfollow, mute, or block accounts that regularly post unlawful content.
  • 3 Scroll past unlawful content immediately without lingering.
  • 4 Replace time spent on haram content with Qur'ān recitation or beneficial media.
  • 5 Make du'ā' for Allah's protection from the fitnah of the eyes.

Chapter Ten

Protecting Modesty in Pictures & Videos

The Advice

Modesty (Ḥayā') in dress, appearance, and behaviour must extend to what a Muslim posts of themselves and their family online. Posting immodest pictures or videos — even for attention or to keep up with trends — is a violation of Islamic principles. A believer's honour and dignity are trusts from Allah and must not be compromised for likes, views, or social validation.

Qur'ān 24:31
وَقُل لِّلۡمُؤۡمِنَٰتِ يَغۡضُضۡنَ مِنۡ أَبۡصَٰرِهِنَّ وَيَحۡفَظۡنَ فُرُوجَهُنَّ وَلَا يُبۡدِينَ زِينَتَهُنَّ إِلَّا مَا ظَهَرَ مِنۡهَا
سورة النور، الآية ٣١
"Tell the believing women to lower their gaze and guard their chastity, and not to display their adornment except that which ordinarily appears."
Ḥadīth — Modesty is part of faith
الْحَيَاءُ شُعْبَةٌ مِنَ الْإِيمَانِ
"Modesty is a branch of faith."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (9) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (35)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ زَيِّنَّا بِزِينَةِ الْإِيمَانِ وَاجْعَلْنَا هُدَاةً مُهْتَدِينَ
Allāhumma zayyinnā bi-zīnatil-īmān waj'alnā hudātan muhtadīn
"O Allah, adorn us with the adornment of faith and make us guides who are guided."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Apply the same modesty standards online that you would in public.
  • 2 Do not post pictures that reveal 'awrah or that could invite fitna.
  • 3 Think carefully before posting images of children or family members.
  • 4 Do not follow trends that compromise Islamic modesty standards.
  • 5 Remember: what is posted online is very difficult to fully remove.

Chapter Eleven

Avoiding Shameful & Immoral Trends

The Advice

Social media is driven by trends — many of which are shameful, immoral, or frivolous. A Muslim must have the strength of character to resist participating in trends that contradict Islamic values, even when everyone around them is doing so. True confidence lies not in following the crowd but in following Allah's guidance. Participating in immoral trends normalises sin and damages one's relationship with Allah.

Qur'ān 24:19 — Do not spread immorality
إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ يُحِبُّونَ أَن تَشِيعَ ٱلۡفَٰحِشَةُ فِي ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ لَهُمۡ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ فِي ٱلدُّنۡيَا وَٱلۡأٓخِرَةِ
سورة النور، الآية ١٩
"Indeed, those who like that immorality should be spread among those who have believed — for them is a painful punishment in this world and the Hereafter."
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْنِي أَبْعُدُ عَنِ السُّوءِ وَالْمَعَاصِي
Allāhumma ij'alnī ab'udu 'anis-sū' wal-ma'āṣī
"O Allah, keep me far from evil and sinful acts."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Do not participate in viral challenges that involve immodesty, mockery, or harm.
  • 2 Do not share, like, or comment on content promoting immoral behaviour.
  • 3 Be firm in your values even when peers pressure you to follow trends.
  • 4 Counter harmful trends by creating and promoting beneficial content instead.

Chapter Twelve

The Danger of Showing Off on Social Media

The Advice

Social media is the most fertile ground for riyā' (showing off) in the modern age. Posting acts of worship, charity, or piety for public admiration corrupts good deeds and can nullify their reward. A Muslim must examine their intentions carefully before every post, especially those involving worship or religious acts. Sincere deeds done privately carry more weight than public displays done for approval.

Qur'ān 107:4–6 — Showing off in worship
فَوَيۡلٌ لِّلۡمُصَلِّينَ ۝ ٱلَّذِينَ هُمۡ عَن صَلَاتِهِمۡ سَاهُونَ ۝ ٱلَّذِينَ هُمۡ يُرَآءُونَ
سورة الماعون، الآيات ٤–٦
"Woe to those who pray — those who are heedless of their prayer — those who show off."
Ḥadīth — Riyā' is minor shirk
أَخْوَفُ مَا أَخَافُ عَلَيْكُمُ الشِّرْكُ الْأَصْغَرُ: الرِّيَاءُ
"The thing I fear most for you is the minor shirk: showing off."
📚 Musnad Aḥmad (23630)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ أُشْرِكَ بِكَ شَيْئًا وَأَنَا أَعْلَمُ
Allāhumma innī a'ūdhu bika an ushrika bika shay'an wa anā a'lam
"O Allah, I seek refuge in You from associating anything with You knowingly."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Keep some acts of worship private — what Allah sees is enough.
  • 2 Examine your intention: are you sharing to inspire others, or for praise?
  • 3 If you notice you post only when people will see, reduce public sharing of worship.
  • 4 Do not post charity, prayer, or fasting for public recognition.

Chapter Thirteen

Protecting the Heart from Envy & Pride

The Advice

Social media is a constant comparison machine — displaying others' achievements, wealth, beauty, and lifestyles. This breeds hasad (envy) and kibr (pride) in the heart, both of which are deeply destructive. A Muslim must guard their heart against envy by being grateful to Allah, and against pride by recognising that all blessings come from Him alone. Social media should never become a benchmark for one's worth or a cause of jealousy.

Ḥadīth — Envy destroys good deeds
إِيَّاكُمْ وَالْحَسَدَ فَإِنَّ الْحَسَدَ يَأْكُلُ الْحَسَنَاتِ كَمَا تَأْكُلُ النَّارُ الْحَطَبَ
"Beware of envy, for envy devours good deeds just as fire devours wood."
📚 Sunan Abū Dāwūd (4903) — Ḥasan
Ḥadīth — No arrogance shall enter Paradise
لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ مَنْ كَانَ فِي قَلْبِهِ مِثْقَالُ ذَرَّةٍ مِنْ كِبْرٍ
"No one who has an atom's weight of arrogance in his heart will enter Paradise."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (91)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ طَهِّرْ قَلْبِي مِنَ الْكِبْرِ وَالْحَسَدِ وَالْغِلِّ
Allāhumma ṭahhir qalbī minal-kibr wal-ḥasad wal-ghil
"O Allah, purify my heart from pride, envy, and malice."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 When you feel envy at someone's post, immediately make du'ā' for them and say "Māshā'Allāh."
  • 2 Reduce time on feeds that trigger comparison and dissatisfaction.
  • 3 Count your own blessings frequently — gratitude is the antidote to envy.
  • 4 Do not flaunt blessings online in ways that cause others pain.

Chapter Fourteen

Managing Time & Avoiding Social Media Addiction

The Advice

Time is one of the greatest blessings Allah has given — and it will be accounted for on the Day of Judgement. Social media platforms are engineered to maximise the time we spend on them. A Muslim must take deliberate control over their time online, setting boundaries and limits, lest hours intended for worship, family, and learning are consumed by mindless scrolling. Discipline with time is an act of gratitude to Allah.

Qur'ān 103:1–3
وَٱلۡعَصۡرِ ۝ إِنَّ ٱلۡإِنسَٰنَ لَفِي خُسۡرٍ ۝ إِلَّا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ وَعَمِلُواْ ٱلصَّٰلِحَٰتِ
سورة العصر، الآيات ١–٣
"By time, indeed mankind is in loss — except those who believe and do righteous deeds."
Ḥadīth — Youth and free time will be questioned
لَا تَزُولُ قَدَمَا عَبْدٍ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ حَتَّى يُسْأَلَ عَنْ عُمُرِهِ فِيمَا أَفْنَاهُ
"A servant's feet will not move on the Day of Judgement until he is asked about his life and how he spent it."
📚 Jāmi' at-Tirmidhī (2417) — Ḥasan Authentic
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْعَجْزِ وَالْكَسَلِ
Allāhumma innī a'ūdhu bika minal-'ajzi wal-kasal
"O Allah, I seek refuge in You from weakness and laziness."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Set daily screen time limits for social media apps.
  • 2 Establish phone-free times: during prayer, meals, and before sleep.
  • 3 Turn off non-essential notifications to reduce compulsive checking.
  • 4 Replace mindless scrolling time with Qur'ān, dhikr, or beneficial learning.
  • 5 Perform a weekly review: how many hours did you spend online, and for what?

Chapter Fifteen

Balancing Social Media with Worship & Study

The Advice

Social media must never take priority over worship, learning, and responsibilities. When prayer times pass while a person is scrolling, or when Qur'ān is left unread while hours are spent on apps, something has gone deeply wrong. A Muslim's first obligations are to Allah, then to knowledge, then to family and work. Social media, at best, occupies the time that remains after these priorities are met.

Qur'ān 4:103 — Prayer at fixed times
إِنَّ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ كَانَتۡ عَلَى ٱلۡمُؤۡمِنِينَ كِتَٰبٗا مَّوۡقُوتٗا
سورة النساء، الآية ١٠٣
"Indeed, prayer has been decreed upon the believers at fixed times."
Ḥadīth — Seeking knowledge is obligatory
طَلَبُ الْعِلْمِ فَرِيضَةٌ عَلَى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ
"Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim."
📚 Sunan Ibn Mājah (224) — Authentic
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ أَعِنِّي عَلَى ذِكْرِكَ وَشُكْرِكَ وَحُسْنِ عِبَادَتِكَ
Allāhumma a'innī 'alā dhikrika wa shukrika wa ḥusni 'ibādatika
"O Allah, help me to remember You, to thank You, and to worship You in the best manner."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Never delay a prayer because of social media — put the phone down at the adhān.
  • 2 Allocate fixed daily time for Qur'ān before opening any app.
  • 3 Keep social media out of study and work hours.
  • 4 Use social media itself as a source of Islamic learning where possible.

Chapter Sixteen

Choosing Good Friends & Beneficial Accounts Online

The Advice

Just as the companions we keep offline shape our character, the accounts we follow online shape our thinking, values, and behaviour. Following scholars, students of knowledge, beneficial content creators, and Islamic organisations enriches the soul and strengthens faith. Following heedless, immoral, or harmful content erodes it. A Muslim must curate their online environment intentionally and wisely.

Ḥadīth — You are upon the religion of your close friend
الْمَرْءُ عَلَى دِينِ خَلِيلِهِ فَلْيَنْظُرْ أَحَدُكُمْ مَنْ يُخَالِلُ
"A person is upon the religion of his close friend, so let each of you look carefully at whom he befriends."
📚 Sunan Abū Dāwūd (4833) — Ḥasan Authentic
Qur'ān 18:28 — Keep good company
وَٱصۡبِرۡ نَفۡسَكَ مَعَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَدۡعُونَ رَبَّهُم بِٱلۡغَدَوٰةِ وَٱلۡعَشِيِّ يُرِيدُونَ وَجۡهَهُۥ
سورة الكهف، الآية ٢٨
"And keep yourself patient with those who call upon their Lord morning and evening, seeking His Face."
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْنِي مَعَ مَنْ تُحِبُّ وَتَرْضَى

"O Allah, place me among those whom You love and are pleased with."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Follow accounts of scholars, Islamic educators, and beneficial content creators.
  • 2 Periodically audit your following list and unfollow what does not benefit you.
  • 3 Join online Islamic communities and study groups.
  • 4 Engage with beneficial content by sharing and commenting positively.

Chapter Seventeen

Avoiding Bad Company & Harmful Influencers

The Advice

Influencers and content creators who promote haram lifestyles, immoral behaviour, and worldly excess can have a profound negative impact on those who follow them. A Muslim must recognise that the digital people they spend time with — even passively — influence their values and desires. Avoiding such company online is an extension of the Prophetic guidance to choose righteous companions.

Qur'ān 43:67 — Some friendships become enmity
ٱلۡأَخِلَّآءُ يَوۡمَئِذِۭ بَعۡضُهُمۡ لِبَعۡضٍ عَدُوٌّ إِلَّا ٱلۡمُتَّقِينَ
سورة الزخرف، الآية ٦٧
"Close friends, that Day, will be enemies to each other — except for the righteous."
Ḥadīth — The blacksmith and the perfume seller
مَثَلُ الْجَلِيسِ الصَّالِحِ وَالْجَلِيسِ السَّوْءِ كَحَامِلِ الْمِسْكِ وَنَافِخِ الْكِيرِ
"The example of a good companion and a bad companion is like the seller of musk and the blacksmith."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (5534) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (2628)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ اصْرِفْ عَنِّي أَصْحَابَ السُّوءِ وَاهْدِنِي لِصُحْبَةِ الصَّالِحِينَ
Allāhumma iṣrif 'annī aṣḥāba as-sū', wahdinī liṣuḥbatiṣ-ṣāliḥīn
"O Allah, keep bad companions away from me and guide me to the company of the righteous."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Unfollow influencers who normalise haram behaviour, materialism, or immodesty.
  • 2 Do not romanticise or admire lifestyles that contradict Islamic values.
  • 3 Be aware of subtle influence — repeated exposure gradually shifts values.
  • 4 Replace harmful content with Islamic content that strengthens your faith.

Chapter Eighteen

Using Social Media for Da'wah & Spreading Good

The Advice

Social media presents an unprecedented opportunity for da'wah — inviting people to Allah. A single beneficial post, Qur'ānic reminder, or clarification of a misconception can reach thousands. Every Muslim has a responsibility to contribute positively to the online space, spreading truth and countering falsehood with wisdom and beautiful conduct. Da'wah online must be done with sincerity, accuracy, and the noble character of the Prophet ﷺ.

Qur'ān 16:125 — Call to the way of your Lord
ٱدۡعُ إِلَىٰ سَبِيلِ رَبِّكَ بِٱلۡحِكۡمَةِ وَٱلۡمَوۡعِظَةِ ٱلۡحَسَنَةِۖ
سورة النحل، الآية ١٢٥
"Invite to the way of your Lord with wisdom and good instruction."
Ḥadīth — Whoever guides to good earns its reward
مَنْ دَلَّ عَلَى خَيْرٍ فَلَهُ مِثْلُ أَجْرِ فَاعِلِهِ
"Whoever guides to something good will have a reward equal to that of the one who does it."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (1893)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْنَا دُعَاةً إِلَى الْخَيْرِ وَأَعِنَّا عَلَى نَشْرِ دِينِكَ
Allāhumma ij'alnā du'ātan ilal-khayr wa a'innā 'alā nashri dīnik
"O Allah, make us callers to good and help us in spreading Your dīn."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Share Qur'ānic verses, ḥadīth, and Islamic reminders regularly.
  • 2 Be a source of accurate Islamic information — verify before you share.
  • 3 Engage in da'wah with patience, wisdom, and good character — not harshness.
  • 4 Represent Islam well in all your content — you are an ambassador of the dīn.
  • 5 Make sincere du'ā' that Allah uses your platform for His cause.

Chapter Nineteen

The Manners of Posting, Sharing & Commenting

The Advice

Islamic manners (ādāb) must govern every aspect of how a Muslim interacts online. Before posting, consider: is this true? Is it beneficial? Is it kind? Before sharing, consider: have I verified this? Could this cause harm? Before commenting, consider: is my tone respectful? Does my contribution add value? Good online manners are an extension of good character and reflect the teachings of the Prophet ﷺ.

Ḥadīth — The best among you in character
إِنَّ مِنْ أَحَبِّكُمْ إِلَيَّ وَأَقْرَبِكُمْ مِنِّي مَجْلِسًا يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ أَحَاسِنَكُمْ أَخْلَاقًا
"The most beloved of you to me and the closest to me in position on the Day of Judgement are those best in character."
📚 Jāmi' at-Tirmidhī (2018) — Ḥasan Authentic
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ حَسِّنْ خُلُقِي كَمَا حَسَّنْتَ خَلْقِي
Allāhumma ḥassin khuluqī kamā ḥassanta khalqī
"O Allah, beautify my character as You have beautified my form."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Apply the three-step test before any post: True? Beneficial? Kind?
  • 2 Begin posts that teach or advise with "Bismillāh" and good manners.
  • 3 Thank those who share beneficial content with you.
  • 4 Avoid chain messages, excessive tagging, or spamming.
  • 5 Correct errors gracefully if you have made mistakes in your posts.

Chapter Twenty

Protecting Privacy & Personal Dignity Online

The Advice

Islam greatly values privacy — for oneself and for others. Sharing personal details, family matters, or private conversations online can expose a person to harm, exploitation, and loss of dignity. A Muslim should be discreet in what they share about themselves and strictly avoid exposing the private affairs of others. The Prophet ﷺ taught that concealing others' faults is a means of Allah concealing one's own on the Day of Judgement.

Ḥadīth — Whoever conceals a Muslim's fault
مَنْ سَتَرَ مُسْلِمًا سَتَرَهُ اللَّهُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ
"Whoever conceals a Muslim's fault, Allah will conceal his fault on the Day of Judgement."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (2442) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (2580)
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ اسْتُرْ عَوْرَاتِنَا وَآمِنْ رَوْعَاتِنَا
Allāhumma ustur 'awrātinā wa āmin raw'ātinā
"O Allah, conceal our faults and grant us security from our fears."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Do not share private family or personal matters publicly online.
  • 2 Never screenshot or share private conversations without permission.
  • 3 Review your privacy settings regularly on all platforms.
  • 4 Do not expose others' private lives, struggles, or sins online.
  • 5 Be careful what personal information you share with strangers online.

Chapter Twenty-One

Repentance from Online Sins & Digital Misconduct

The Advice

Every Muslim makes mistakes — and the digital world provides many opportunities for them. Whether it is a hurtful comment, shared falsehood, immodest post, or time wasted in sin, the door of repentance (Tawbah) is always open. A Muslim should not despair; they should act immediately — deleting harmful content, rectifying wrongs done to others, and turning sincerely to Allah. Allah is Most Merciful and loves those who return to Him.

Qur'ān 39:53 — Do not despair of Allah's mercy
قُلۡ يَٰعِبَادِيَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَسۡرَفُواْ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمۡ لَا تَقۡنَطُواْ مِن رَّحۡمَةِ ٱللَّهِۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَغۡفِرُ ٱلذُّنُوبَ جَمِيعًاۚ
سورة الزمر، الآية ٥٣
"Say: O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed, Allah forgives all sins."
Ḥadīth — The best sinners are those who repent
كُلُّ بَنِي آدَمَ خَطَّاءٌ وَخَيْرُ الْخَطَّائِينَ التَّوَّابُونَ
"All the children of Adam commit errors, and the best of those who err are those who repent."
📚 Jāmi' at-Tirmidhī (2499) — Ḥasan Authentic
Du'ā' for This Chapter
اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي وَتُبْ عَلَيَّ إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ التَّوَّابُ الرَّحِيمُ
Allāhumma ighfir lī wa tub 'alayya innaka anta at-tawwābur-raḥīm
"O Allah, forgive me and accept my repentance. Indeed, You are the Accepter of repentance, the Most Merciful."
Practical Guidance
  • 1 Delete or correct any harmful or sinful content you have posted.
  • 2 Apologise to anyone you have wronged online.
  • 3 Make sincere repentance to Allah with regret and intention to change.
  • 4 Follow every online sin with an online good deed.
  • 5 Never allow past mistakes to lead to despair — Allah's mercy is greater.

Chapter Twenty-Two

Using Social Media as a Means to Paradise, Not Regret

The Advice

The ultimate goal of a Muslim is Paradise. Every aspect of life — including how we use social media — is either drawing us closer to that goal or pulling us away from it. A Muslim who approaches social media with consciousness of Allah, sincerity, discipline, and good character can transform it into a means of earning immense reward. But one who uses it carelessly, sinfully, and without accountability risks standing before Allah with a record full of regret. The choice is made with every post, every scroll, every click.

Qur'ān 16:97 — A good life for the righteous
مَنۡ عَمِلَ صَٰلِحٗا مِّن ذَكَرٍ أَوۡ أُنثَىٰ وَهُوَ مُؤۡمِنٌ فَلَنُحۡيِيَنَّهُۥ حَيَوٰةٗ طَيِّبَةٗۖ وَلَنَجۡزِيَنَّهُمۡ أَجۡرَهُم بِأَحۡسَنِ مَا كَانُواْ يَعۡمَلُونَ
سورة النحل، الآية ٩٧
"Whoever does righteousness, male or female, while a believer — We will surely grant him a good life, and will surely give them their reward in the Hereafter according to the best of what they used to do."
Ḥadīth — The most beloved deeds are consistent ones
أَحَبُّ الْأَعْمَالِ إِلَى اللَّهِ أَدْوَمُهَا وَإِنْ قَلَّ
"The most beloved of deeds to Allah are the most consistent, even if they are small."
📚 Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (6464) · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (783)
Closing Du'ā'
اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْنِي مِنَ الْمُسْتَهْدِينَ إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ وَنَجِّنَا مِنَ النَّارِ وَاجْعَلْ أَعْمَالَنَا خَالِصَةً لِوَجْهِكَ الْكَرِيمِ
Allāhumma ij'alnī minal-mustahdīn ilal-jannah wa najjinā minan-nār, waj'al a'mālanā khāliṣatan liwajhikal-karīm
"O Allah, make me among those guided to Paradise and save us from the Fire, and make our deeds sincere for Your Noble Face."
Final Practical Guidance
  • 1 Before opening any app, pause and make intention: "I use this for what pleases Allah."
  • 2 Perform consistent small good deeds online — even one beneficial post a day.
  • 3 Regularly reflect: is my online life drawing me closer to Allah or further away?
  • 4 Use social media as a platform for justice, truth, and good — not vanity and sin.
  • 5 Keep the Hereafter in mind with every click, post, and share.
  • 6 Make du'ā' that on the Day of Judgement, your digital record is a source of joy, not regret.
Social media is a test. Used with taqwā, sincerity, and good character, it becomes a path to Paradise. Used carelessly, it becomes a path to regret. The Muslim chooses — with knowledge, intention, and the help of Allah.
﴾ وَاللَّهُ يَهۡدِي مَن يَشَآءُ إِلَىٰ صِرَٰطٖ مُّسۡتَقِيمٍ ﴿

"And Allah guides whom He wills to a straight path." — Qur'ān 2:213
